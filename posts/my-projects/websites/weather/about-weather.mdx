## Accuracy and Reliability

Understanding weather forecast limitations helps set appropriate expectations:

**Current Conditions**: Highly accurate, typically reflecting real-time measurements from nearby weather stations with minimal delay.

**Short-Term Forecasts** (1-3 days): Generally very reliable, with accuracy rates typically above 80-90% for temperature and conditions.

**Medium-Term Forecasts** (4-7 days): Good reliability for general trends, though specific details like exact precipitation timing may be less precise.

**Extended Forecasts** (8-16 days): Provide useful trend information for planning purposes, but specific predictions become less certain as the forecast period extends. Best used for general planning rather than precise scheduling.

**Microclimates**: Weather can vary significantly over short distances due to topography, urban heat islands, and other local factors. Forecasts represent general area conditions and may differ from hyperlocal conditions.
